# Music Refresh Wave

## Summary

This wave refreshes the non-menu soundtrack to increase contrast and reduce
fatigue during long campaign sessions.

The sacred menu theme remains unchanged:

- `DRIFT SIGNAL`

The world anchor themes remain the emotional pillars:

- `GENESIS PULSE`
- `HOLLOW SIGNAL`
- `CURRENT`

The remaining gameplay tracks were rewritten to feel brighter, more melodic,
and more forward-moving without breaking the established procedural score layer.

## Direction Applied

### Preserved

- menu identity
- one anchor theme per world
- grouped soundtrack routing by campaign context

### Refreshed

- `SIEGE BLOOM`
  - more momentum
  - brighter push energy
  - less oppressive loop feel
- `ECHO CORE`
  - stronger melodic identity
  - more decisive and less generic pressure
- `ENTROPY CURRENT`
  - more movement and lift
  - less dark drift repetition
- `OBLIVION GATE`
  - still weighty, but more ceremonial and memorable
- `SIGNAL WAR`
  - more drive, confidence, and coalition pressure
- `TRANSCENDENCE PROTOCOL`
  - more final-surge energy and less mechanical grind

## Player-Facing Notification Change

Music notifications now present:

- track title
- a short emotional description of the track's soul

They no longer show:

- BPM
- loop duration
- technical audio metadata

  - World 2 phases `15-20`
- `OBLIVION GATE`
  - World 2 phase `21`
- `CURRENT`
  - World 3 tutorial and phases `23-24`

  - campaign ending

## Rationale

The previous grouped soundtrack was structurally sound, but too emotionally
homogeneous. This wave keeps the grouped model while improving contrast between:

- opening vs pressure phases
- world anchors vs support tracks
- boss/finale energy vs ordinary pressure loops

The result should be a campaign that breathes more and feels less sonically
fatiguing over repeated play.

## Rhythm Refresh Follow-Up

A second pass also increased rhythmic contrast between the refreshed tracks.

The main change was not harmony, but groove identity:

- `SIEGE BLOOM`
  - more forward-leaning gallop
- `ECHO CORE`
  - more decisive boss drive
- `ENTROPY CURRENT`
  - more broken and unstable pulse
- `OBLIVION GATE`
  - more ceremonial march weight
- `SIGNAL WAR`
  - more urgent coalition pressure
- `TRANSCENDENCE PROTOCOL`
  - more relentless finale propulsion

This keeps the grouped soundtrack structure, while making the cadence itself
less homogeneous from track to track.
