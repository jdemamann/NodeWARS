# AI Wave — Tactical Pressure Expansion

## Executive Read

This wave was focused on one product goal:

> make the enemy feel more dynamic, more dangerous, and less numerically flat without turning the AI into a heavy planner

The implementation was intentionally constrained to the existing AI architecture:

- keep heuristics lightweight
- keep slice on the canonical shared gameplay path
- keep all new tuning in `gameConfig.js`
- improve visible tactical behavior before attempting deeper planning systems

---

## What Changed

### 1. Enemy slice pressure became real

The coalition can now convert charged lanes into tactical burst cuts instead of relying almost entirely on ordinary throughput.

Key properties:

- slice still routes through `Tent.applySliceCut(...)`
- purple remains the more permissive slice user
- red can also cut, but only under much stricter thresholds
- slice attempts are gated by faction-specific cooldowns

This makes slice a real enemy threat instead of a mostly hidden purple edge case.

### 2. The AI now uses lightweight tactical states

The AI derives one of five tactical profiles before scoring moves:

- `expand`
- `pressure`
- `support`
- `finish`
- `recover`

This changes how target categories are valued without introducing a full planner.

### 3. Player structural weakness is now scored explicitly

The AI now reads and punishes:

- isolated player expansions
- weakly supported player nodes
- overextended nodes with too many outgoing lanes
- low-energy fronts that are easy to collapse

This improves perceived intelligence more than raw stat inflation.

### 4. Purple faction identity is broader and clearer

Purple now feels more like:

- opportunistic
- lethal
- tempo-oriented
- willing to convert charged lanes into pressure spikes

Red remains more reliable and structurally oppressive.

## Expected Player-Facing Impact

### What should feel better

- enemy turns create more tempo swings
- weak player fronts should be punished more intentionally
- purple should feel more distinct and threatening
- late-game pressure should come more from enemy behavior and less from blunt numeric weight

### What should not change

- AI still is not a hidden-information planner
- slice still uses the same canonical path as the player
- coalition rules between red and purple stay coherent
- relays still require real budget to matter

## What To Watch In Playtest

The next useful observations are not architectural. They are product-facing:

### Watch for positive signals

- purple creates memorable burst moments
- the enemy punishes obvious overextension
- AI feels less monotone phase-to-phase

### Watch for regressions

- too many enemy cuts in rapid succession
- red feeling too similar to purple
- AI over-focusing already doomed targets
- support behavior becoming too conservative in `recover`

---

## Recommended Next Step

The next best move is not more AI architecture immediately.

The next best move is a short playtest-and-tune pass focused on:

- phases with purple pressure
- relay-heavy maps
- late authored maps where tempo swings matter most

That should decide whether this wave needs:

- threshold tuning only
- or a follow-up extraction of slice-candidate construction into its own helper
