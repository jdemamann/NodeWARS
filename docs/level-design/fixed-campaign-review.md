# Fixed Campaign Review

## Goal

Define a phase-by-phase level-design review for the current campaign and identify which levels must become fixed/authored to deliver a high-quality player experience.

This document evaluates:

- pedagogical purpose
- difficulty role
- frustration risk
- procedural suitability
- authored-layout priority

## Design Principle

A level should be fixed/authored when one or more of these are true:

- it introduces a new mechanic
- it is a boss or capstone test
- its identity depends on chokepoints, route structure, or timing windows
- unfair procedural variance would undermine learning

Procedural or semi-procedural levels are acceptable when:

- the lesson is broad rather than positional
- the map does not rely on specific lane structure
- variance adds replay value without confusing player learning

## Campaign-Wide Assessment

Current campaign structure is strong at the macro level:

- World 1 teaches expansion and efficiency
- World 2 teaches spatial denial and risk routing
- World 3 teaches infrastructure control and multi-objective pressure

The main weakness is that many phases have strong names and intended identities, but still rely on procedural generation. That makes the actual player experience less teachable and less fair than the campaign structure suggests.

## Recommended Campaign Structure

Use 3 content classes:

### 1. Fully Authored

Use for:

- tutorials
- first appearance of a new mechanic
- boss levels
- high-identity tactical maps

### 2. Semi-Authored

Use for:

- fixed core lanes/objectives with some neutral-node variation
- support levels between major mechanic milestones

### 3. Parametric/Procedural

Use for:

- lower-stakes support levels
- broad pacing levels without heavy positional learning requirements

## Design Requirements For Fixed Levels

Every fully authored level should explicitly define:

- player spawn position
- enemy spawn positions
- central objective
- first 20-second intended decision
- comeback vector
- fail condition most players will hit first
- one mechanic the level teaches or tests
